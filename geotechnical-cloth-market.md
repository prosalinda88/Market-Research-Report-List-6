<p><p>Geotechnical cloth can be classified into two main types: synthetic and natural. Synthetic geotechnical cloth is made from man-made materials such as polyester, polypropylene, and polyethylene. These materials offer high tensile strength, durability, and resistance to environmental factors such as UV rays and chemicals. On the other hand, natural geotechnical cloth is made from materials like jute, coir, and sisal. These materials are biodegradable and environmentally friendly, making them a sustainable choice for erosion control and soil stabilization applications.</p></p>
