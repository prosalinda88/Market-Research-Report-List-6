<p><p>The inorganic metal finishing market encompasses various processes designed to enhance metal surfaces. Anodizing involves creating a protective oxide layer on aluminum, improving corrosion resistance. Cladding refers to bonding a thin layer of metal over another for durability. Conversion coatings provide a thin protective layer that improves adhesion and corrosion resistance. Electroplating applies a metal coating through electrolysis for enhanced appearance and protection, while electroless plating uses a chemical reaction for coating without electricity. Other methods may include chemical treatments or specialty coatings.</p></p>
