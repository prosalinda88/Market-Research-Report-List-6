<p><p>The Optical Fiber Current Sensor (OFCS) market is primarily divided into two types: AC (Alternating Current) and DC (Direct Current) sensors. AC OFCS is designed to measure varying current flows in applications like power distribution and electrical equipment monitoring, utilizing the principles of magnetic fields affecting light propagation. Conversely, DC OFCS targets steady current measurements, crucial for renewable energy systems and battery management. Both types leverage optical fiber technology for enhanced performance, accuracy, and resistance to electromagnetic interference.</p></p>
