<p><p>The noise-cancelling headphones market is typically divided into two main types: active and passive noise-cancelling headphones. Active noise-cancelling headphones use built-in microphones to detect ambient sounds and generate opposing sound waves to cancel out noise, offering an immersive listening experience. In contrast, passive noise-cancelling headphones rely on physical design elements, such as cushioned ear pads, to block out external noise by creating a seal around the ears. Both types cater to different user preferences and environments.</p></p>
