<p><p>Gas-filled detectors are devices that utilize a gas-filled chamber to detect and measure ionizing radiation. These detectors operate by allowing the radiation to ionize the gas within the chamber, creating charged particles that can be collected and measured to produce a signal proportional to the radiation present. Common types of gas-filled detectors include ionization chambers, proportional counters, and Geiger-Müller counters, which are widely used in various sectors, including healthcare, nuclear power, environmental monitoring, and security.</p><p>The Gas-Filled Detectors Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.2% during the forecast period.
