<p><p>Orthostatic hypotension drugs primarily include Midodrine, Northera (droxidopa), Fludrocortisone, and other medications. Midodrine is an alpha-agonist that increases vascular tone, helping to raise blood pressure upon standing. Northera converts to norepinephrine, enhancing blood flow and pressure. Fludrocortisone is a corticosteroid that promotes sodium retention and fluid balance. Other drugs may encompass various formulations and treatments aimed at managing symptoms of orthostatic hypotension, assisting patients in maintaining stability and preventing dizziness upon position changes.</p></p>
