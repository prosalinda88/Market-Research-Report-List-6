<p><p>The chocolate market is divided into two primary types: real chocolate and compound chocolate. Real chocolate includes dark, milk, and white varieties made with cocoa solids, cocoa butter, and sugar. Dark chocolate has a higher cocoa content and is often less sweet, while milk chocolate contains milk solids for creaminess. White chocolate lacks cocoa solids, focusing on cocoa butter and sugar. In contrast, compound chocolate substitutes cocoa butter with cheaper vegetable fats, offering a different texture and taste profile, often used in coatings and confections.</p></p>
