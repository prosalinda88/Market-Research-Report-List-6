<p><p>Polymer materials used for drug packaging include various types, each with distinct properties. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is renowned for its durability and flexibility, making it suitable for blister packaging. PE (polyethylene) is lightweight and resistant to moisture, often used in bags and pouches. PET (polyethylene terephthalate) offers excellent clarity and barrier properties, ideal for bottles. PP (polypropylene) is heat-resistant and versatile, commonly used in containers. Other materials may include biodegradable options, catering to eco-friendly packaging needs in the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
