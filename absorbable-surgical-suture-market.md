<p><p>The absorbable surgical suture market comprises synthetic and natural sutures, each with distinct characteristics. Synthetic sutures, such as poliglecaprone (Monocryl), vicryl, and polydioxanone (PDS), are designed for specific applications, offering reliable tensile strength and predictable absorption rates. Natural sutures, including collagen and catgut, are derived from animal sources, providing a bio-compatible option but may vary in absorption time. Polyglycolic acid sutures represent another synthetic choice, known for their smooth handling and controlled absorption, making them widely used in various surgical procedures.</p></p>
