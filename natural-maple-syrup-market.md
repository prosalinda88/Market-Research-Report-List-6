<p><p>The natural maple syrup market features various types of syrup derived from different species of maple trees. Acer saccharum, or sugar maple, is known for its high sugar content and produces the sweetest syrup, making it the most commercially popular choice. Acer nigrum, or black maple, also yields a rich syrup with a similar flavor profile. Acer palmatum, or Japanese maple, is less common in syrup production, typically offering a lighter flavor, appealing to niche markets seeking unique tastes.</p></p>
