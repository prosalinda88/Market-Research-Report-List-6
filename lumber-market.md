<p><p>The lumber market is primarily divided into two main types: softwood and hardwood. Softwood lumber comes from coniferous trees such as pine and spruce, characterized by its light weight, ease of handling, and suitability for construction, furniture, and paper products. In contrast, hardwood lumber originates from deciduous trees like oak and maple, known for its density and durability, making it ideal for high-quality furniture, flooring, and cabinetry. Both markets cater to distinct consumer needs and varying applications in construction and woodworking.</p></p>
